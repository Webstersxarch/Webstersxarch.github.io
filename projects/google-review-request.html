THE PROBLEM

Happy customers rarely leave reviews on their own.

01 — TIMING

Ask for a review the moment a job wraps and it gets lost under the invoice, the cleanup, the next appointment.

02 — ONE SHOT

Most businesses send one request and stop. If it's not opened, that review — and that social proof — is just gone.

03 — NO TRACKING

Without knowing who actually clicked the link, there's no way to tell who's worth a gentle nudge and who's already left one.

04 — MANUAL FOLLOW-UP

Remembering to check back in three days, for every job, on top of everything else — that's the kind of task that never happens.

HOW IT WORKS

Two workflows, timed three days apart, running without a reminder.

A

Detect

The moment a job's status is marked complete in the tracking sheet, the workflow picks it up automatically.

B

Request

A review request email goes out right away, with a tracked link back to the Google review page.

C

Wait 3 days

The workflow pauses, then re-reads that same row to see whether the review link was ever clicked.

D

Follow up

No click on record? A second, softer nudge goes out. Already clicked? The row is left alone — no double-messaging.

WHAT'S UNDER THE HOOD

Built on n8n — self-checking, no manual re-checking required

ENGINE

n8n, self-hosted or n8n Cloud — the whole request-and-follow-up sequence can run on your own infrastructure or a managed instance, whichever fits how you hand it off.

TRIGGER

Google Sheets — watches the job tracker for rows flipped to "Complete" and starts the sequence instantly.

DELIVERY

Gmail sends the initial review request with a unique tracked link tied back to that customer's row.

TRACKING

A webhook records when the review link is clicked, so the sheet always reflects real customer activity — not a guess.

TIMING

A built-in wait step holds for three days, then re-reads the row before deciding whether a follow-up is needed.
